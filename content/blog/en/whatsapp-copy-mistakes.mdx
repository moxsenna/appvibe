Many businesses already have a good WhatsApp button, but the moment a prospect opens the chat, everything falls apart. The first message sent often makes people go silent — or worse, block you.

Here are the 5 most common WhatsApp copy mistakes — and how to fix them.

## 1. Selling immediately without context

**Bad:**
```
Hello, this is Healthy Clinic. We have a special acne treatment promo this month. Want to book?
```

**Better:**
```
Hi, I saw you asked about acne on our Instagram. Is there anything I can help explain?
```

**Why it fails:** The person doesn’t feel recognized. Starting with a sale feels like spam.

## 2. Writing walls of text

**Bad:**
```
Good afternoon, thank you for contacting Healthy Clinic. We are a specialist skin clinic established since 2018 and have served more than 5,000 patients. Currently we have a special promotion for acne treatment...
```

**Better:**
```
Good afternoon! How can I help you with our acne treatment?
```

**Why it fails:** People read on their phones. They want quick answers, not a presentation.

## 3. Using overly formal or stiff language

**Bad:**
```
We would like to inform you that our dermatologist consultation schedule is available every Tuesday and Thursday from 10:00–16:00 WIB.
```

**Better:**
```
Our dermatologist is available Tuesday & Thursday, 10:00–16:00. Would you like me to check available slots?
```

**Why it fails:** Stiff language makes the conversation feel cold. People prefer natural tone.

## 4. Not giving the next clear step

**Bad:**
```
Thank you. We will wait for your further response.
```

**Better:**
```
Got it. Would you like me to send this week’s available schedule, or do you have other questions first?
```

**Why it fails:** Without a clear next step, the conversation dies right there.

## 5. Ignoring the prospect’s pain or problem

**Bad:**
```
We have the best acne treatment in the city.
```

**Better:**
```
Dealing with acne that won’t go away can be really frustrating. Our treatment usually shows visible results after 3–4 weeks. Would you like me to explain the process?
```

**Why it fails:** People don’t care how great your treatment is. They care whether you understand their problem.

## First WhatsApp message checklist

- [ ] Mentions context (where they came from)
- [ ] Short (maximum 2–3 sentences)
- [ ] Gives a choice or clear next step
- [ ] Uses natural language
- [ ] Touches on the prospect’s problem or desire

## Ready-to-use message examples

**Clinic / Healthcare**
```
Hi, I saw you asked about acne. Is there anything I can help explain about our treatment?
```

**Property**
```
Hi, thank you for viewing our unit. Is there anything you’d like me to explain about the price or floor plan?
```

**Consulting / Education**
```
Hi, I saw you’re interested in our mentoring program. Would you like me to send a quick overview first?
```

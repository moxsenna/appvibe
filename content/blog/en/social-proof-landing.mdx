Many landing pages display testimonials, but not all of them create real impact. Some are too generic, some are too long, and some even make visitors more skeptical.

Good social proof is not about having “many testimonials,” but about **credibility** and **relevance** to the person viewing the page.

## Types of Social Proof That Work Best

### 1. Testimonials with name + photo + specific results

**Weak:**
> “The service was excellent.” — Anonymous

**Stronger:**
> “After 3 sessions, my acne reduced by 80%. I now feel much more confident during presentations.”  
> — Rina Wijaya, Marketing Manager, Jakarta

### 2. Specific numbers and statistics

Strong examples:
- “Helped 2,400+ clients since 2021”
- “Average reply time: 18 minutes”
- “92% of clients continued to the next package”

These numbers create a sense of safety because they are measurable.

### 3. Client logos or media coverage

If your business has been featured in the media or worked with recognizable clients, display their logos. This works especially well for B2B or professional services.

### 4. Short case studies (1 paragraph + results)

Instead of just testimonials, tell a short story:
- The problem the client faced
- The solution you provided
- The result they achieved

## Common Mistakes When Displaying Social Proof

- Testimonials that are too long (people rarely read them)
- No photo or full name
- Using testimonials from people who are not relevant to your target audience
- Showing too many testimonials at once (overwhelming)

## Real Examples We Have Implemented

**Skin Clinic**
- Displayed 3 testimonials with photos + before/after results (not just text)
- Added “Trusted by 1,800+ patients in Greater Jakarta”

**Premium Residential Property**
- Showed logos of media that covered their projects
- Displayed one short case study: “The Pratama family bought a 3BR unit after only 2 visits”

**Education / Mentoring Program**
- Showed photos of participants + their current job titles
- Added “87% of participants received a promotion within 6 months”

## Social Proof Checklist

- [ ] Every testimonial includes a name + photo (or initials + city)
- [ ] Results mentioned are specific (not just “good”)
- [ ] No more than 4–5 testimonials on the main page
- [ ] At least one supporting statistic or number
- [ ] Social proof matches the audience visiting the page
